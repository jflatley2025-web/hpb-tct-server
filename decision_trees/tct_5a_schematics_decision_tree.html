The Four TCT Schematics at a Glance
›
Core rule: Every TCT schematic is a three-tap model — no more, no fewer. The range extreme is always tapped exactly three times before the breakout resolves. Entry is always a BOS. Target is always the opposite range extreme (Wyckoff High/Low).

Model 1 Accumulation LONG

  • T1
    Original Range Low (Tap 1)
  • T2
    Deviation below Range Low — deeper (Tap 2). DL2 must hold.
  • T3
    Deviation below Tap 2 — deepest (Tap 3). DL2 must hold.
  • ↑
    BOS bullish from Tap 2–3 structure → Enter Long. SL below T3. Target: Range High (Wyckoff High).

Model 2 Accumulation LONG

  • T1
    Original Range Low (Tap 1)
  • T2
    Deviation below Range Low (Tap 2). DL2 must hold.
  • T3
    Higher Low (does NOT take out T2). Must grab extreme liquidity OR mitigate extreme demand.
  • ↑
    BOS bullish from Tap 2–3 structure → Enter Long. SL below T3. Target: Range High (Wyckoff High).

Model 1 Distribution SHORT

  • T1
    Original Range High (Tap 1)
  • T2
    Deviation above Range High — higher (Tap 2). DL2 must hold.
  • T3
    Deviation above Tap 2 — highest (Tap 3). DL2 must hold.
  • ↓
    BOS bearish from Tap 2–3 structure → Enter Short. SL above T3. Target: Range Low (Wyckoff Low).

Model 2 Distribution SHORT

  • T1
    Original Range High (Tap 1)
  • T2
    Deviation above Range High (Tap 2). DL2 must hold.
  • T3
    Lower High (does NOT take out T2). Must grab extreme liquidity OR mitigate extreme supply.
  • ↓
    BOS bearish from Tap 2–3 structure → Enter Short. SL above T3. Target: Range Low (Wyckoff Low).

Prerequisite: Confirm the Range
›
No range = no schematic. All standard range rules from Lecture 2 apply: six-candle rule, equilibrium touch, DL2 drawn. The schematic only begins after the range is confirmed.
  • Trend direction identified (uptrend = Distribution context; downtrend = Accumulation context)
  • Fibonacci drawn in correct direction (downtrend: bottom→top; uptrend: top→bottom)
  • Six-candle rule passes on the working timeframe
  • Price has returned to the equilibrium (0.5 Fib) — range confirmed
  • DL2 levels drawn at −0.3 / 1.3 (30% extension above Range High / below Range Low)
  • Identify the Wyckoff High (highest point of the full schematic) and Wyckoff Low (lowest point)

3
Identify Schematic Direction: Accumulation or Distribution?
›
Which range extreme is being repeatedly tested/deviated?
→ The Range Low is being deviated (price going below)
ACCUMULATION schematic — sell-side liquidity is being grabbed. Market maker filling long positions. Expect break upward. Target: Range High / Wyckoff High.
→ The Range High is being deviated (price going above)
DISTRIBUTION schematic — buy-side liquidity is being grabbed. Market maker filling short positions. Expect break downward. Target: Range Low / Wyckoff Low.
Re-accumulation / Re-distribution: The prior trend direction tells you if it is a reversal or continuation, but the schematic mechanics are identical. Trade the same way regardless of prior context.
4
Validate Tap 2 — First Deviation (DL2 Rule)
›
Tap 1 = the original Range Low (Accumulation) or Range High (Distribution). It is the range extreme itself — no validation needed beyond the confirmed range.
Has price moved beyond the Range Low (Acc.) or Range High (Dist.) for the first time?
✓ Yes — price exceeded the range extreme
Apply DL2 check below.
✗ No — range extreme not yet tested
Wait. Tap 2 cannot form until the range extreme is first reached.
Did any candle close beyond the DL2 (30% extension)?
✗ No — wick only, or close stayed within DL2
Tap 2 is valid. The deviation held within limits. Extend the range extreme to this new Tap 2 level. Recalculate DL2 from the new Tap 2 extreme.
✓ Yes — candle closed beyond DL2
Tap 2 is INVALID. This is a true break, not a deviation. The schematic is invalidated. Reassess macro structure.
Did price accept back inside the range after the Tap 2 deviation?
✓ Yes
Tap 2 confirmed. Proceed to Phase 5 (determine Model 1 or 2).
✗ No — price still outside
Wait for acceptance back inside. Do not proceed without it.
5
Anticipate Model 1 or Model 2? (Do Not Force — Confirm)
›
"You don't trade expectations, you trade confirmations." — TCT Lecture 5A
At Tap 2, did price grab massive liquidity on the left AND/OR mitigate a strong supply/demand zone?
✓ Yes — large liquidity grab or strong S/D mitigation at Tap 2
Model 2 is likely — the market maker may have filled enough position at Tap 2 with no incentive to deviate again. Anticipate Model 2, but wait for confirmation. If the structure doesn't break and price takes out Tap 2, switch to Model 1.
✗ No — Tap 2 did not grab significant liquidity or mitigate S/D
Model 1 is likely — the MM probably needs another deviation to fill his position. Wait for a Tap 3 that goes deeper/higher than Tap 2.
Decision rule: Always wait. Never enter during or after Tap 2. The schematic is not yet complete. Only enter on the BOS after Tap 3 is confirmed.
6
Validate Tap 3 — Model 1 or Model 2?
›
Did price exceed the Tap 2 extreme (go deeper/higher than Tap 2)?
✓ Yes — new deeper/higher deviation
Potential Model 1 Tap 3. Apply DL2 check (no close beyond DL2 from the new Tap 2 reference). If valid → Tap 3 confirmed for Model 1. Proceed to Phase 7.
✗ No — price stopped short of Tap 2 (higher low / lower high)
Potential Model 2 Tap 3. Must pass Model 2 validation below.
Model 1 Tap 3 Validation
Did the Tap 3 deviation close beyond DL2 (recalculated from Tap 2 extreme)?
✗ No — within DL2
Model 1 Tap 3 VALID. Proceed to Phase 7.
✓ Yes — closed beyond DL2
Model 1 Tap 3 INVALID. True break — schematic invalidated. Reassess.
Model 2 Tap 3 Validation — Must Pass At Least ONE
RequirementAccumulation (higher low)Distribution (lower high)
A — Extreme Liquidity Tap 3 grabs the first Market Structure Low (six-candle rule) from Tap 2 upward toward the highest point between T2 and T3.
Exception: if first MSL is extremely close to the range low → use second MSL instead.
Low-TF exception (1m/2m/3m): if a pivot misses one candle for the rule but looks significant → still valid.		 Tap 3 grabs the first Market Structure High (six-candle rule) from Tap 2 downward toward the lowest point between T2 and T3.
Same exceptions apply.
B — Extreme Demand/Supply Tap 3 mitigates the last order block or structure demand zone between current price and the Tap 2 low. Preferably in the extreme discount (bottom 25% of range). Zone TF must be proportional to range size. Tap 3 mitigates the last order block or structure supply zone between current price and the Tap 2 high. Preferably in the extreme premium (top 25% of range). Zone TF must be proportional to range size.
Does the Model 2 Tap 3 candidate meet Requirement A OR Requirement B (or both)?
✓ Yes — at least one requirement met
Model 2 Tap 3 VALID. Both met = extra confluence. Proceed to Phase 7.
✗ No — neither requirement met
Model 2 Tap 3 INVALID. This is not a tradeable Tap 3. Do not enter. Continue watching for Model 1 (price may take out Tap 2 next).
Model 2 liquidity rationale: Traders who entered short at Tap 2 trail stop losses down to the first MS High. Taking that MSH on Tap 3 sweeps all those trailing stop losses (buy-side liquidity) — enabling the MM to fill more shorts before reversing down.

Proceed to Phase 8 to check BOS location (inside vs. outside range values).
✗ No — BOS has NOT occurred
Do not enter. "You trade confirmations, not expectations." The schematic is still forming. Wait for the BOS.

8
Check BOS Location & Choose Entry Timeframe
›
Did the BOS (break of structure) occur inside the original range values (between original Range Low and Range High)?
✓ Yes — BOS inside the original range values
Best case. High confidence entry. Enter on this BOS.
✗ No — BOS outside the original range values
Riskier. "It's always better to wait for a break of structure that's inside the range." Still valid but lower confidence. Enter with smaller size or wait for confirmation to return inside.
Is the higher-TF (black) BOS inside the original range values but giving a poor R:R (e.g., near equilibrium)?
✓ Yes — black BOS is deep inside range (poor R:R)
Multi-TF Entry Refinement:
Check if the last expansion into Tap 3 produced a mid-TF (red) market structure. If the red BOS is also inside the original range values → enter on the red BOS instead.

Benefit: dramatically better R:R (example: 2.75R on red BOS vs. 1:1 on black BOS).
Risk: monitor whether black structure also confirms. If red structure breaks bullish (wrong direction) before taking the black-level low — be cautious, but note this bounce often represents price mitigating the supply left behind by the BOS candle, then resuming the move.
✗ No — black BOS gives acceptable R:R
Enter on the black (higher-TF) BOS as primary.
Supply left behind: After a bearish BOS in a Distribution schematic, a small bounce upward is typically price mitigating the structural supply created by the BOS candle — then it continues down. Not a true invalidation on mid/lower timeframes.

Beyond Target "Anything beyond is extra" — hold if broader context (HPB, main structure) supports continuation Same logic — broader context determines whether to hold beyond Wyckoff Low
Wyckoff High vs Range High (Re-accumulation)
In a re-accumulation context (uptrend continuing up), the Range High drawn on the Fibonacci may differ from the Wyckoff High (the actual highest point of the schematic structure including Tap 2 and Tap 3 highs).
Target the Wyckoff High, not the Range High, when they differ.
Solution: draw the range locally from the lowest point of the schematic to the highest point to align them.
Same applies to re-distribution: target the Wyckoff Low, not the Range Low.
10
Pre-Trade Checklist — All Must Pass
›
  • Range confirmed (six-candle rule + EQ touch + DL2 drawn)
  • Schematic direction identified (Accumulation = Range Low being deviated; Distribution = Range High being deviated)
  • Context confirmed (Accumulation/Re-accumulation or Distribution/Re-distribution)
  • Tap 1 identified (original Range Low or Range High)
  • Tap 2 validated: deviation within DL2 + accepted back inside range. Range extreme extended to Tap 2. DL2 recalculated.
  • Model 1: Tap 3 is a new deeper/higher deviation, also within DL2.
  • Model 2: Tap 3 is a higher low / lower high meeting Requirement A (extreme liquidity) OR B (extreme demand/supply). Zone TF proportional to range. Demand in extreme discount / supply in extreme premium preferred.
  • Market structure drawn from highest/lowest point between Tap 2 and Tap 3 toward Tap 3 extreme
  • BOS confirmed in the correct direction from that structure
  • BOS location checked — inside original range values preferred; outside is riskier
  • Multi-TF entry considered if black BOS gives poor R:R (use red BOS if inside range values)
  • Stop loss placed beyond Tap 3 extreme
  • Target set to Wyckoff High / Wyckoff Low (not just Range High / Range Low if they differ)

Model 2 → Model 1 Transition (Most Common Loss Scenario)
Sequence:
1. Tap 2 forms. Expect Model 2 (large liquidity grab at Tap 2).
2. Tap 3 candidate forms as higher low / lower high. Requirement A or B appears met.
3. BOS forms in the wrong direction → or price fails to break structure → or price takes out Tap 2.
4. Model 2 is invalidated. Stop loss hit.
5. The new deviation beyond Tap 2 = potential Model 1 Tap 3.
6. Apply Model 1 Tap 3 validation (DL2 check).
7. Wait for Model 1 BOS. Re-enter on confirmation.

"Often times when the Model 2 fell, it's because it wants to be a Model 1."
