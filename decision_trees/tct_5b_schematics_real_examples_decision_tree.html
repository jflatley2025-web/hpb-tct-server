Step 0: The Rationality Rule — Does This Actually Look Like a Range?
›
"I don't want you guys to be doing that. I want you guys to be rational. A range is just horizontal price action." — TCT Lecture 5B
Does the price structure between the range extreme and its deviations look like horizontal consolidation?
✓ Yes — horizontal/sideways price action, deviations are wicks or "bad breaks"
Rational range. Clean TCT schematic candidate. Proceed to Phase 2.
✗ No — massive impulsive/explosive candles, not consolidating
Irrational / not a range. Do not force tap counts onto impulsive moves. Skip — wait for genuine horizontal price action to develop.

Key tell: In every real example from this lecture, taps were either clean wicks or "bad breaks" (candle closed above/below, immediately came back). When you see price literally going sideways like horizontal price action — that is the ideal TCT schematic shape.

Step 1: Identify the Highest Valid Timeframe for the TCT Schematic
›
Always do this at the first deviation (Tap 2). The highest valid TF label tells you which schematic you're trading and matters for the advanced lectures. It determines which demand/supply zone timeframes are proportional.
Starting from your analysis timeframe, step up through TFs (30m → 45m → 1H → 2H → 3H → 4H → 6H…). On each TF, does the six-candle rule still apply across the range AND the first deviation?
✓ Yes — six-candle rule holds on this TF
Continue stepping up to the next higher TF. Keep checking.
✗ No — six-candle rule breaks on this TF
The previous TF was your highest valid TF. Label the schematic by that timeframe (e.g., "45-minute TCT schematic", "4-hour TCT schematic"). Use this TF to calibrate demand/supply zone sizes.
Six-Candle Rule Quick Check (per TF)
What to verify at each TFPass criteria
Range formation: high → range lowAt least 2 consecutive bearish (or bullish) closing candles trading in the range direction
Range confirmation: back to EQAt least 2 consecutive candles in the opposite direction returning to equilibrium
First deviation from range extremeAt least 2 consecutive candles completing the deviation pattern
All three conditions above must holdIf any one fails → that TF is not valid → step back to previous TF

Step 2: Tap Spacing Check — Are the Three Taps Reasonably Spread?
›
Is the distance between Tap 2 and Tap 3 extremely small compared to the distance between Tap 1 and Tap 2?
✓ Yes — Tap 2–3 gap is extremely close / negligible
Suspect Tap 3. Prefer taps to be somewhat equally spaced. If Tap 3 doesn't even appear as a distinct swing on the schematic's own timeframe, it is NOT a valid Tap 3 on that TF.
Check: zoom out to the schematic's highest valid TF. Can you see a distinct swing at the Tap 3 level? If not → invalid Tap 3. The schematic is still forming.
✗ No — Tap spacing is reasonable / somewhat equal
Tap spacing acceptable. Continue to Tap 3 validation.
Rule: "Preferably you want your taps to be somewhat more or equally spread out." A Tap 3 that is barely a blip on the schematic's TF is not a real Tap 3 — you cannot draw valid six-candle structure from it. This is especially important when lower-TF schematics approach higher-TF range extremes.

Step 3 (Model 2): Extreme Liquidity vs Extreme S/D — Which to Prioritize?
›
Recap from 5A: Model 2 Tap 3 must meet Requirement A (extreme liquidity grab) OR Requirement B (extreme demand/supply mitigation). From 5B real examples, here is how to weigh them when they conflict or one is much clearer.
Is one of the two requirements (extreme liquidity or extreme S/D) dramatically more obvious and clean than the other?
✓ Yes — one is very obvious, the other is weak or unclear
Focus on the obvious one. "If one of the two is so obvious and looks so clean, just watch that one." Both aligning is ideal but not required — the quality of the requirement matters more than checking both boxes.
✗ No — both are of similar clarity (or both weak)
If both are clear → extra confluence, high confidence.
If both are weak → reconsider whether this is a valid Model 2 Tap 3.
Trendline Liquidity — A Specific Extreme Liquidity Form
Trendline liquidity = a series of successively lower highs (distribution) or higher lows (accumulation) creating a clean diagonal liquidity trail. When price sweeps across all those highs/lows in one move, it is grabbing massive buy-side or sell-side liquidity.

If a trendline liquidity trail is obvious on the left of Tap 3's area AND the first MSH/MSL aligns with that trail → this is the most obvious extreme liquidity possible. Focus on it exclusively even if no supply/demand zone is present.

Step 4: Assess R:R Before Entry — Do You Need to Go Lower TF?
›
If you were to enter on the main (higher-TF / black) BOS with SL at Tap 3 extreme and target at Wyckoff High/Low — what is the R:R?
→ R:R is ≥ 1.5 and target is the Wyckoff High/Low
Acceptable. Enter on the main BOS. No need to go lower TF.
→ R:R is < 1 or entry would be near equilibrium
Do NOT take this entry. A sub-1:1 R:R on a valid setup means you entered too late in the Tap 2–3 structure. Apply overlapping structure / domino effect (Phase 6) to find a lower-TF entry.
→ R:R is borderline (1.0–1.5)
Consider going lower TF for a better entry. Weigh the schematic quality. On very high TF schematics (4H+), a 1:1 on the primary BOS may still be acceptable given the magnitude.

Step 5: Overlapping Structure / Domino Effect — Getting a Better Entry
›
When to use: When the main (black) BOS gives poor R:R because the market structure break is too far from the Wyckoff target, dissect the last expansion leg (from the highest/lowest point between Tap 2 and Tap 3 toward Tap 3 extreme) using lower-TF structure layers.

Go to Red target

Break Red (mid TF)

Go to Black target

Break Black (high TF)
→
Wyckoff Target
Can you identify red (mid-TF) or blue (low-TF) market structure inside the last expansion leg toward Tap 3?
✓ Yes — valid structure layers found
Proceed to entry rule below. Check the critical condition for each lower-TF structure break.
✗ No — no valid lower-TF structure inside the leg
No lower-TF refinement available. Wait for the main (black) BOS even with poor R:R, or skip the trade if R:R is unacceptable.
For Model 1 schematics: Is the lower-TF (red or blue) BOS back inside the original range values?
✓ Yes — lower-TF BOS is inside original range values
Valid lower-TF entry. Enter on the lower-TF BOS. SL at Tap 3 extreme. Take profit targets: lower-TF target (next structure level), then mid-TF, then Wyckoff High/Low. Watch for each level to cascade.
✗ No — lower-TF BOS is outside original range values
Riskier. Step down one more TF to find a BOS that is inside the original range values. If none found → wait for the main BOS.
For Model 2 schematics: Does the lower-TF BOS look reasonable given the demand/supply zone was already mitigated?
✓ Yes — looks clean, demand/supply clear, no obvious obstruction
Valid lower-TF entry. Enter on lower-TF BOS. This is especially useful when the main BOS would give <0.5R.
✗ No — supply/demand zone directly above/below BOS candle (obstruction)
Apply the Reconfirmation Tool (Phase 7). Do NOT enter directly on the BOS if it occurs inside or directly below/above a supply/demand zone.
How to Dissect a Structure Leg — Practical Steps
  1. Identify the last expansion leg from highest/lowest point between T2–T3 → toward T3 extreme.
  2. On the schematic's primary TF: draw structure (black). If BOS gives poor R:R → go lower.
  3. On mid TF (red): draw structure inside that same leg. If BOS is inside original range values → entry candidate.
  4. On lower TF (blue): draw structure inside the red structure's last leg. Apply the same BOS inside range check.
  5. Never go below the point where structure is meaningful. Use six-candle rule at each level.

Step 6: The Reconfirmation Tool — Never Enter on a Break Inside S/D
›
"You never want to enter on a break that's in a supply zone. You always want to see if we're going to surpass it and you'll enter on the retest." — TCT Lecture 5B
Is the BOS candle (the candle that breaks the market structure) occurring inside or immediately adjacent to a supply zone (Distribution) or demand zone (Accumulation)?
✗ No — BOS is in clear space, not in a S/D zone
Enter normally on the BOS. No reconfirmation needed.
✓ Yes — BOS candle is inside a S/D zone
Do NOT enter on this BOS. The break may be a false break or the zone will resist immediately. Apply Reconfirmation Tool below.
Reconfirmation Tool Process
After the BOS candle, did price pull back (retest the BOS level)?
✓ Yes — price pulled back after BOS
Apply lower-TF structure check below.
✗ No — price never pulled back after BOS
Wait. If price never retests, you simply missed the entry. Do not chase. The schematic is still valid — wait for the next structure opportunity.
On the lower TF (blue): during the pullback, is price making a higher low (Accumulation) or lower high (Distribution) in the correct direction?
✓ Yes — lower TF shows higher low / lower high and then BOS in schematic direction
Reconfirmation confirmed. Enter on the lower-TF BOS confirmation of the higher low / lower high. SL at Tap 3 extreme.
✗ No — lower TF breaks in wrong direction (blue breaks against schematic)
Reconfirmation failed. Blue structure never confirmed the pullback as a higher low. Do NOT enter. The BOS was likely a false break. Keep watching — if price later meets Model 1 conditions (takes Tap 2 extreme), switch to Model 1 protocol.
Real example (45min M2 Acc → M1): Structure broke in the supply zone. Multiple wicks into the zone but no blue structure confirmation. Model 2 eventually failed. Became a Model 1 Accumulation. Entering on the Model 1 BOS gave 3.3R. The lesson: the reconfirmation tool saved traders from a false entry AND revealed the Model 1 was forming.

Bonus: Range Formation Signal — Lower-TF Break Up + Immediate New Low
›
"When you break back to bullish but take the low one more time — it is a typical sign of a range formation where it's a bigger range." — TCT Lecture 5B
Price made a lower-TF bullish BOS (break back to bullish) but then immediately took the previous low again?
✓ Yes — bullish break followed by new lower low
Range formation signal. Zoom out to the next higher TF. On the higher TF, this entire sequence collapses into a single downward impulse — the local bullish break was internal noise, not a real reversal. Look for a bigger range to be forming on the higher TF. Apply range rules from the beginning (new Fib, EQ touch, etc.).
✗ No — BOS held (no new lower low)
The BOS is likely genuine. Continue monitoring for range development or directional continuation.

Full Pre-Trade Checklist — 5A + 5B Combined
›
Foundation (from 5A)
  • Range confirmed: six-candle rule + EQ touch + DL2 drawn
  • Schematic direction identified (Range Low deviated = Acc; Range High deviated = Dist)
  • Tap 1 identified (original range extreme)
  • Tap 2 validated: within DL2, accepted back inside range. Range extreme extended. DL2 recalculated.
  • Model 1 or 2 Tap 3 validated (see 5A decision tree for full checklist)
  • Tap 2–3 structure drawn. BOS confirmed in schematic direction.
New Checks (from 5B)
  • Rationality check: Does the range look like horizontal price action? Deviations are wicks or bad breaks — not massive impulsive moves.
  • Highest TF identified: Walked up TFs, confirmed the highest TF where six-candle rule still applies. Schematic labeled by that TF.
  • Tap spacing: Tap 2–3 gap is reasonably proportional to Tap 1–2. Not extremely compressed. Tap 3 shows as a visible swing on the schematic's TF.
  • Model 2 S/D quality: Assessed whether extreme liq or extreme S/D is more obvious. Zone TF is proportional to schematic TF.
  • R:R calculated: Primary BOS R:R is at least 1.5. If not, overlapping structure identified for better entry.
  • Overlapping structure: If going lower TF for entry — lower-TF BOS is inside original range values (Model 1) or clearly looks reasonable (Model 2).
  • Reconfirmation: If BOS candle is inside a S/D zone — waiting for retest + lower-TF higher low / lower high confirmation. Not entering directly on the BOS.
  • Stop loss: Below Tap 3 low (Acc) or above Tap 3 high (Dist) — regardless of which TF entry is used.
  • Target: Wyckoff High or Wyckoff Low. Confirm it matches Range High/Low or adjust if re-accumulation/re-distribution context.
