TCT Lecture 3 — Supply & Demand Decision Tree

Hybrid documentation + machine-parsable decision tree for the TCT Supply & Demand model.

1
Build Structural Context
›
Always determine market structure before marking supply or demand.
What is the current structural state?
Range → look for supply at premium and demand at discount.
Uptrend → demand zones at higher-low pivots.
Downtrend → supply zones at lower-high pivots.
No clear structure → wait.
2
Identify Zone Type
›
Zone Type Description
Order Block Single candle preceding expansion containing FVG.
Structure Supply / Demand Multi-candle base preceding strong impulse.
3
Confirm Fair Value Gap
›
Does candle 1 wick overlap candle 3 wick?
Yes → no inefficiency → zone invalid.
No → FVG confirmed.
4
Draw Zone Boundaries
›
Use the most extreme wick across OB and adjacent candles.
5
Check Zone Mitigation
›
Was the zone entered and price expanded away?
Yes → zone retired.
No → zone still valid.
6
Refine Across Timeframes
›
Scan HTF → LTF to locate refined OB within broader zone.
7
Zone Priority
›
Priority Zone Type
1 Extreme Supply / Demand
2 Multi-TF Confluence
3 Structure + OB
4 Single OB
8
Wait for Confirmation
›
Do NOT trade the touch. Wait for a TCT schematic inside the zone.
9
Post-Reaction Zone Handling
›
After mitigation + expansion → remove zone.
10
Pre-Trade Checklist
›
  • Structure identified
  • Zone type confirmed
  • FVG exists
  • Zone drawn correctly
  • Zone unmitigated
  • Timeframe refinement complete
  • Zone priority evaluated
  • Price inside zone
  • TCT schematic confirmed
  • Target defined
+
Zone Scanning Workflow
›
  1. Identify structure context
  2. Mark range extremes if applicable
  3. Scan premium for supply
  4. Scan discount for demand
  5. Identify extreme zones
  6. Move HTF → LTF
  7. Find refined OBs
  8. Rank zone priority
  9. Wait for price to reach POI
  10. Watch for TCT schematic

Condition Action

No FVG present Zone invalid
