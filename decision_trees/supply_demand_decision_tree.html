1
Build Structural Context First
›
Rule: Never place supply or demand zones randomly. Every zone must have a structural reason. Always determine market context before marking any zones.
What is the current market context?
→ Price is in a defined Range
Range context:
• Look for Supply in the upper section (premium) of the range and above the Range High
• Look for Demand in the lower section (discount) of the range and below the Range Low
• Also look for "extreme supply" (last supply before range high) and "extreme demand" (last demand before range low) — highest priority zones
→ Price is in a Downtrend (lower highs, lower lows)
Downtrend context:
• Look for Supply at each lower high pivot (where the pullback reverses and price makes a new lower low)
• These are the structural distribution points
→ Price is in an Uptrend (higher highs, higher lows)
Uptrend context:
• Look for Demand at each higher low pivot (where the pullback reverses and price makes a new higher high)
• These are the structural accumulation points
→ No clear structure
Do not place zones. Wait for structure to become clear.
2
Identify Zone Type: Order Block (OB) or Structure Supply/Demand?
›

There are exactly two types of supply/demand zones. Both require a Fair Value Gap (FVG) to be valid.

Zone Type Definition Candle Count FVG Required?
Order Block (OBIF) Supply or demand represented in a single candle + FVG. Acronym: Order Block with Inefficiency. 1 candle (the OB) + 2 surrounding candles to confirm FVG YES — non-negotiable
Structure Supply / Demand Supply or demand represented in multiple candles forming a structural move (e.g., the entire pullback leg before the expansion). Multiple candles YES — non-negotiable
Key rule: "If there's no Fair Value Gap, it's not a valid order block." This applies equally to both zone types. The FVG is what gives the zone trading power.

Do the wick high of Candle 1 and the wick low of Candle 3 fail to connect (leave a gap)?
✓ Yes — they do NOT touch
FVG confirmed. A valid inefficiency exists. Proceed to drawing the zone.
Even a very small gap qualifies — "you just need an inefficiency, you just need one."
✗ No — they touch or overlap
No FVG — zone is INVALID. Do not mark this as an order block or structure zone. There is no inefficiency to trade from.
FVG Edge Cases
ScenarioRuling
FVG is extremely small (barely visible) Still valid — size does not matter, only that the gap exists
Adjacent candle has a wick higher/lower than the OB candle itself Still valid — extend the OB box to include that wick ("grab the wick high/low")
FVG was tapped from the top down (entered from above, not filled from below) Less invalidating — zone retains more validity than a bottom-up fill
FVG fully filled / price traded through it Invalidated — if zone also reacted and expanded away, retire it entirely
4
Draw the Zone Boundaries
›
Bullish Demand OB — Drawing Rules
  1. Identify the last bearish candle before the upward expansion
  2. Confirm the three-candle FVG formation exists above it
  3. Draw the bottom of the box at the wick low of the OB candle (or the adjacent candle if its wick is lower — always use the most extreme wick)
  4. Draw the top of the box at the wick high of the OB candle (or adjacent candle if higher)
Bearish Supply OB — Drawing Rules
  1. Identify the last bullish candle before the downward expansion
  2. Confirm the three-candle FVG formation exists below it
  3. Draw the top of the box at the wick high of the OB candle (or adjacent candle if higher)
  4. Draw the bottom of the box at the wick low of the OB candle (or adjacent candle if lower)
Structure Supply / Demand — Drawing Rules
→ Structure Supply (multiple bullish candles before bearish expansion)
Top: The high of the entire structural move (highest wick)
Bottom: The low of the candle just before the bearish expansion began (where the FVG starts)
→ Structure Demand (multiple bearish candles before bullish expansion)
Top: The high of the structure (highest candle in the move down, capturing the FVG above)
Bottom: The low of the entire structural move (lowest wick)
Wick rule: Always use the most extreme wick across candles 1 and 2 to define the zone boundary — not just the OB candle. "It doesn't matter if it's candle one's wick high or candle two's wick high — we just pull towards the wick high."
5
Check Zone Validity: Is It Mitigated?
›
Has price previously entered this zone, caused a reaction, and expanded away from it?
✓ Yes — fully mitigated
Zone is RETIRED. Do not use this block anymore. "The moment we get a mitigation of our order block within a move away, we do not use it anymore."
✗ No — zone has not been entered yet
Unmitigated zone. Proceed to Phase 6.
→ Partial / borderline mitigation (barely tapped)
Sole-zone exception: If this is the only supply or demand zone in the area AND the mitigation was extremely minor:
• Redraw the zone above the mitigation point (for supply) or below it (for demand)
• Still treat it as unmitigated
Only applies when no other zone exists nearby.
Did price wick through the zone (wick only, no close through it)?
✓ Yes — wick through, no close
Still valid. A wick through the zone means liquidity was grabbed from that point — the zone itself is not invalidated. "Sometimes we wick the order block — that's fine. It just means we took liquidity from that point."
6
Refine the Zone Across Timeframes
›
Can a more precise zone be identified on a lower timeframe within this zone?
✓ Yes — a refined OB exists on a lower TF
Place both zones on the chart — the broad structure zone and the refined OB.
Watch whichever price enters first. You are waiting for TCT confirmations in either zone, not trading the reaction mechanically.
✗ No — only the broad zone exists
Use the broad zone as your primary POI.
Timeframe Scanning Workflow
Scan from higher to lower timeframes: Daily → 18H → 12H → 8H → 6H → 4H → 3H → 2H → 1H → 45m → 30m → 15m

Look for the best (most refined, most recent, unmitigated) OB or structure zone at the relevant structural pivot on each timeframe.
Timeframe Priority Rules
ScenarioResult
Higher TF OB is also unmitigated on lower TF Best case — strong confluence, preferred
Higher TF OB is mitigated on lower TF but not on higher TF Still usable — higher TF structure takes precedence. Not ideal but valid.
Lower TF OB only (no higher TF confluence) Usable — lower confidence, rely on TCT confirmation quality
Supply chain rule: When OB₁ creates OB₂ which creates OB₃, the second OB in the chain tends to produce the strongest reaction. Flag supply chains when price returns toward them.
7
Assess Zone Priority: Is This an Extreme Zone?
›
Is this the last remaining unmitigated supply before the Range High, or the last remaining unmitigated demand before the Range Low?
✓ Yes — Extreme Supply or Extreme Demand
EXTREME ZONE  Highest priority zone. These "love to give reactions" and are where new ranges tend to form.
Trade this zone before any other in the area. Expect stronger, faster reactions.
✗ No — regular supply or demand zone
Standard zone priority. Assess confluence and proceed.
Zone Priority Hierarchy
PriorityZone TypeReason
1st Extreme Supply / Extreme Demand Last remaining zone before range extreme. Causes new ranges to form here.
2nd Higher TF OB + Lower TF OB confluence (both unmitigated) Multi-timeframe confluence. Strong structural and FVG confluence simultaneously.
3rd Structure zone containing a refined OB (broad + refined) Dual-zone confluence. Both levels provide a reaction target.
4th Single-layer OB (unmitigated, valid FVG) Valid but single-layer. Quality depends on TCT confirmation.
Supply chain 2nd OB Second OB in an OB chain "The reaction from that second order block always tends to be very good."
8
Wait for Price to Enter the Zone — Then Confirm with TCT Schematic
›
Critical rule: "We are NOT trading the reaction of the order block. We are watching for confirmations inside the order block which will be TCT schematics." The zone is the where. The TCT schematic is the when and how.
Has price entered the zone (supply or demand)?
✓ Yes — price is inside the zone
Watch for a TCT schematic to form inside the zone.
Do not enter on the touch alone. Wait for the confirmation model (TCT Model 1 or Model 2 — covered in Lectures 5–6).
✗ No — price has not reached the zone
Wait. The zone is your Point of Interest (POI). Do nothing until price arrives.

9
After Reaction: Retire or Keep the Zone?
›
Did price enter the zone, react, AND then expand away from it?
✓ Yes — full mitigation + expansion
Retire this zone permanently. Remove it from the chart. "After a mitigation and an expansion away from it, we do not use the block anymore."
✗ No — price entered but did NOT expand away meaningfully
Zone is potentially still valid. Reassess whether a new OB has formed at a more refined level, and whether the FVG within the zone has been filled.
→ Price entered but only wicked through (no close through)
Zone is still valid. The wick was a liquidity grab — the zone itself was not consumed.
10
Pre-Trade Checklist — All Must Pass
›
  • Structural context established (range / uptrend / downtrend)
  • Zone type identified (OB or structure supply/demand) with contextual reason
  • FVG confirmed — wick of candle 1 does NOT connect to wick of candle 3
  • Zone drawn correctly — using most extreme wicks across candles 1 and 2
  • Zone is unmitigated (has not been entered, reacted from, and expanded away)
  • Timeframe scan complete — best refined zone identified and placed
  • Zone priority assessed (extreme / multi-TF confluence / single layer)
  • Price has entered the zone (POI reached)
  • TCT schematic confirmation received inside the zone (Model 1 or Model 2)
  • Target identified (opposite range extreme or next structural pivot)
+
Zone Scanning Workflow (Full Sequence)
›
  1. Identify the current market context — range, uptrend, or downtrend
  2. If range: mark Range High and Range Low (from Lecture 2 process)
  3. Look for supply above Range High and demand below Range Low (for deviation setups)
  4. Scan upper section of range for supply zones (OB + structure supply)
  5. Scan lower section of range for demand zones (OB + structure demand)
  6. Flag "extreme supply" (last unmitigated supply before Range High) and "extreme demand" (last before Range Low)
  7. For trending markets: at each pivot point (lower high or higher low), check for valid OB with FVG
  8. Cycle through timeframes (daily → 15m) to find the most refined valid zone at each POI
  9. Place all valid, unmitigated zones on the chart
  10. Mark zone priority (extreme > multi-TF confluence > structure + OB > single OB)
  11. Wait for price to reach a POI, then watch for TCT confirmation before entering
  12. After a mitigated zone reacts and price expands away → remove it from the chart
