Concept 1: Distributions ↔ Accumulations — Schematic Flip
›
Target Rule ▸ Flip Awareness ▸ Risk Triggers
"A distribution can always go over into an accumulation, just like an accumulation can go over into a distribution." — TCT Lecture 6

When a schematic hits its target (opposite range extreme), price does not have to continue further. Instead it can reverse — creating an opposing schematic in the same range. Understanding when to expect this and how to manage your trade around it is the core skill of Concept 1.

Step 1 — Did Both Sides of the Range Deviate BEFORE Your Schematic Confirmed?
Before your schematic's BOS was confirmed, had the opposite range extreme already been deviated?
✗ No — only the confirmed schematic's side was deviated
Standard targeting: You can freely target the full Wyckoff High/Low (opposite range extreme). If the target is hit, watch for the opposing side to start deviating — it may begin a new schematic. No special handling needed during the trade.
✓ Yes — opposite range extreme was ALREADY deviated before confirmation
Flip-aware mode. Do NOT simply target the full opposite range extreme point. The prior deviation of the opposite side means requirements for an opposing schematic may already be partially met (Tap 1 and Tap 2 already exist for the opposing model). Apply Step 2 below.
Step 2 — Flip-Aware Mode: Watch the Opposing Structure
As price moves toward your target (opposite range extreme), is structure on the opposing side (the side that was already deviated) breaking in the direction of an opposing schematic?
✗ No — opposing structure NOT breaking (no BOS in wrong direction)
Continue holding your trade toward the opposite range extreme. Your schematic is still valid. Opposing schematic not yet confirmed.
✓ Yes — structure breaks in the OPPOSITE direction of your trade
This is your Risk-Off / Risk-On trigger simultaneously:
• Risk-Off for your current trade → take profit / close position
• Risk-On for a new opposing schematic → the opposing BOS is your entry confirmation for the new direction

The original schematic's Model 2 (or Model 1) has failed to hit its full target. The opposing schematic is now confirmed.
Step 3 — Supply/Demand Zone Check at the Opposite Side
Is there visible supply (Distribution scenario) or demand (Accumulation scenario) at the range extreme that your price is approaching — the side that was previously deviated?
✓ Yes — supply/demand zone at the previously deviated extreme
Pre-identify this zone as the potential Model 2 Tap 3 validator for the opposing schematic. If price reaches it AND structure breaks against your trade → opposing schematic is confirmed. Be ready to flip.
✗ No — no significant supply/demand at the previously deviated extreme
Lower probability of flip at that exact level. Still possible — watch structure anyway. If structure never breaks against you, hold to full target.

Flip-Aware Pre-Trade Setup
  • Before entering: check if the opposite range extreme has already been deviated
  • If yes: identify extreme demand/supply and extreme liquidity at that opposite extreme
  • Mark those zones as potential Model 2 Tap 3 validators for the opposing schematic
  • During trade: watch structure at the opposite extreme as price approaches
  • If BOS breaks in opposing direction → exit current position, enter opposing schematic
  • If no opposing BOS → hold to full Wyckoff High/Low target as normal
  • After target hit with no prior opposing deviation: watch for new deviations starting on the now-reached extreme

Concept 2: LTF Ranges Growing into HTF Ranges
›
Tap Spacing ▸ TF Escalation ▸ Nested Schematics
"Every start of a high time frame range was created on the lower time frame, just like your higher market structure pools are created by those pivot market structure breaks on the lower time frame." — TCT Lecture 6

What looks like 4–5 taps on the low TF is often just 3 taps on a higher TF. A low-TF schematic that hits its target may simply be one leg of a forming higher-TF range. Knowing when to escalate TF prevents getting stuck counting taps on the wrong timeframe.

Warning Signal: Is Tap Spacing Compressed?
Is the distance between Tap 2 and Tap 3 significantly smaller than the distance between Tap 1 and Tap 2?
✗ No — taps are roughly equally spaced
Normal schematic. No escalation check required. Proceed with standard 5A/5B evaluation.
✓ Yes — Tap 2–3 gap is much smaller than Tap 1–2 gap
LTF → HTF escalation check required. Step up to the next valid TF. Check if Tap 2 and Tap 3 disappear (merge into a single deviation). If they do → you are likely inside a higher-TF range. Perform Step 2 below.
Also triggers when: you count more than 3 taps
Are you counting 4 or more taps in what you think is a single schematic?
✓ Yes — 4+ taps visible
Recheck on a higher TF. "If you see a four tap — one, two, three, four — recheck it. Knowing this information it will probably just be a three tap on a higher time frame." Apply Step 2.
Step 2 — TF Escalation Check
On the next higher valid TF, do Tap 2 and Tap 3 merge into a single deviation (appear as one move)?
✓ Yes — they disappear / merge on the higher TF
You are in a LTF schematic nested inside a forming HTF range.
1. The LTF schematic (already completed or forming) is one sub-move of the HTF range.
2. On the HTF, this merged deviation = one Tap. Find the HTF equivalent of Tap 2 and Tap 3.
3. The HTF Tap 3 will be validated by a proportionally larger supply/demand zone (3H OB for a 4H range, etc.).
4. Trade the HTF schematic on top of the LTF one — do not confuse them.
✗ No — each tap still shows as distinct on the higher TF
This is a legitimate low-TF schematic. No escalation needed. Apply 5A/5B rules normally.

The LTF T2 and T3 become a single deviation on the HTF.

Concept 3: Wyckoff-in-Wyckoff — TCT Schematic Inside Tap 3
›
R:R Multiplier ▸ Local Schematic Confirmation ▸ Earlier Entry
"Our third tap of our initial TCT schematic has its own little TCT schematic. This way we can have an entry confirmation way earlier than the original break of structure level, causing our R:R to improve drastically." — TCT Lecture 6

As price moves toward Tap 3 of the main schematic, that move may form its own local TCT range. Identifying and entering on the local schematic's BOS gives you the same trade at a dramatically higher R:R — while using the same stop loss (main Tap 3 extreme).

When to Look for Wyckoff-in-Wyckoff
Is price currently moving toward Tap 3 of the main schematic (the leg that creates Tap 3)?
✓ Yes — price trending toward Tap 3 extreme
Zoom into a lower TF on this leg. Look for a local range forming. Apply Step 2.
✗ No — still in earlier phase of main schematic
Not yet applicable. Come back to this concept when price is approaching the Tap 3 area.
Step 2 — Identify the Local (Nested) TCT Range
On a lower TF, does the move toward Tap 3 show a local range with its own three-tap structure?
✓ Yes — local range visible with its own taps
Local TCT schematic confirmed as forming. The main Tap 3 area will be one of the taps of this local schematic. Apply Steps 3–5 below to trade it.
✗ No — no local range / no structure in the Tap 3 leg
No Wyckoff-in-Wyckoff available. Fall back to standard entry on the main BOS (from 5A/5B rules).
Step 3 — Validate the Local Schematic
  • Local range confirmed: six-candle rule applies on the lower TF
  • Local Tap 2 validated: deviation within local DL2, accepted back inside local range
  • Local Tap 3 validated: meets Model 1 or Model 2 requirements (using local extreme liq / local S/D)
  • Local supply/demand zone proportional to the local range TF
  • The overall direction of the local schematic matches the MAIN schematic direction (both are Distribution or both Accumulation in the context of Tap 3 exhausting the move)
Step 4 — Entry, Stop Loss, Target
Has the local schematic's BOS been confirmed?
✓ Yes — local BOS confirmed in the correct direction
Enter on the local BOS.
• Stop Loss: Beyond the main schematic's Tap 3 extreme (not the local one — the main one remains the invalidation)
• Target: The main schematic's Wyckoff High / Wyckoff Low (same as if you had entered on the main BOS)
• The local schematic's confirmation doubles as confirmation that the main Tap 3 has been exhausted and the main schematic is now active
✗ No — local BOS not yet confirmed
Wait. Do not enter on anticipation. Only enter on the local BOS confirmation, just as you would wait for any BOS.

Concept 4: Model 1 Flowing into Model 2 — Stop Trail & R:R Doubling
›
Trade Management ▸ Stop Trail ▸ Add-on Position
"Normally when we have a good-looking Model 1 flowing into Model 2 it tends to have follow-through beyond the range extreme point — because it just shows that the market maker is fully positioning himself." — TCT Lecture 6

After a Model 1 accumulation (or distribution) confirms, price may not go straight to the target. It may curve back toward the range extreme it just broke, creating conditions for a Model 2 third tap. When that Model 2 Tap 3 forms, a specific trade management protocol allows you to: de-risk the existing position AND add a new position — effectively doubling R:R.

Step 1 — You Are Already in a Model 1 Trade
Prerequisites: Model 1 accumulation (or distribution) BOS confirmed. You are long (or short). SL is below Tap 3 low (or above Tap 3 high). Target is Range High / Wyckoff High (or Range Low / Wyckoff Low).
After your Model 1 entry, is price curving back toward the range extreme (toward your stop area) without hitting your target?
✓ Yes — price pulling back toward range extreme
Begin Model 2 watch protocol. Apply Step 2.
✗ No — price moving cleanly toward target
Hold the trade. No Model 2 forming. Take profit at Wyckoff High/Low normally.
Step 2 — Does the Pullback Meet Model 2 Tap 3 Requirements?
Does the pullback level meet at least one of: extreme liquidity grab OR extreme demand/supply mitigation (proportional zone)?
✗ No — pullback meets neither requirement
This is NOT a valid Model 2 Tap 3. Do not adjust the trade. Hold with original stop. If price takes out your Tap 3 → stop hit, trade closed.
✓ Yes — pullback meets extreme liq or extreme demand/supply
Potential Model 2 Tap 3 forming. This pullback is now the new "Wyckoff Point" candidate — the new Tap 3. Apply Step 3.
Step 3 — Wait for Model 2 BOS Confirmation
Has the Tap 2–3 structure (from the highest/lowest point between M1 entry area and the M2 Tap 3) broken in the schematic direction?
✗ No — BOS not yet confirmed
Wait. Do not adjust the trade yet. BOS is the only confirmation — no BOS, no action.
✓ Yes — Model 2 BOS confirmed
Execute the three-step management protocol:

① The new Model 2 Tap 3 low (for accumulation) is your new Wyckoff Low — the invalidation point. If this new Tap 3 low is above your original entry → your stop loss can move to your entry (you are now de-risked).

② Trail the stop loss on your existing position to entry (or to just below the new Tap 3 low, whichever makes sense). Freed-up risk = the difference between original % risk and new % risk at the tighter stop.

③ Add a new position on the Model 2 BOS with the freed-up risk amount (re-deploy the same 1% risk on the new position). Your average entry improves and total R:R approximately doubles.
Step 4 — Extended Target (M1 → M2 Follow-Through)
Does the M1 → M2 upgrade setup have strong confluence (obvious demand/supply, obvious liquidity, clean schematic on both LTF and HTF)?
✓ Yes — high quality M1 → M2 setup
Expect follow-through beyond the Range High/Low. The MM has now grabbed liquidity/demand/supply on BOTH Model 1 deviations AND the Model 2 Tap 3 — leaving nothing unmitigated. This is maximum MM involvement = expect continuation beyond the target.

Extended target options: liquidity curve above/below, unmitigated structure supply/demand beyond Wyckoff High/Low, or the next significant HTF supply/demand zone.
✗ No — average quality / one requirement met weakly
Take profit at the standard Wyckoff High/Low. Do not extend target. Secure the improved R:R from the position management.

Advanced Pre-Trade Checklist — Lecture 6 Additions
›
Before Entering Any TCT Trade (add to 5A/5B checklist)
  • Flip awareness: Has the opposite range extreme been deviated? If yes, identify extreme S/D and extreme liq there. Mark as the opposing schematic's potential Tap 3 zone.
  • LTF/HTF check: Is Tap 2–3 gap much smaller than Tap 1–2? Zoom up one TF — do taps merge? If yes, find the HTF schematic instead.
  • Wyckoff-in-Wyckoff scan: As price approaches Tap 3, is a local range forming in the Tap 3 leg? If yes, validate the local schematic and enter on local BOS (main SL/target unchanged).
  • Context for extended target: Is this a M1 → M2 upgrade scenario developing? If yes, prepare stop trail and add-on plan at M2 BOS confirmation.
  • Follow-through expectation: Is this a clean M1 → M2 with obvious demand/supply + liq + good HTF confluence? If yes, extend target beyond Wyckoff High/Low to liq curve / unmitigated HTF supply/demand.
During Trade Management
  • Monitor for opposing BOS at the opposite range extreme (Concept 1) — if triggered, exit and flip
  • Monitor for M1 → M2 pullback meeting extreme liq/demand requirements (Concept 4)
  • When M2 BOS confirmed: trail SL on existing position to entry, add new position with freed-up risk
  • When extended target reached: scale out or fully close based on remaining liquidity/supply/demand beyond
