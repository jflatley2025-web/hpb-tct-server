1
Identify the Macro Trend Direction
›
Is price making higher highs and higher lows on your working timeframe?
✓ Yes
UPTREND  You will draw the range Fibonacci from Top → Bottom (high to low).
✗ No — price is making lower highs and lower lows
DOWNTREND  You will draw the range Fibonacci from Bottom → Top (low to high).
? Price structure is unclear
Step up one timeframe and re-assess. Do not force a range on ambiguous structure.
Rule: Direction of the Fibonacci pull is non-negotiable. An uptrend = top to bottom. A downtrend = bottom to top. Flipping the direction produces the wrong EQ, premium, and discount zones.
2
Confirm the Range (Six Candle Rule + Equilibrium Touch)
›
Six Candle Rule — minimum structure required to validate a range on any timeframe
Candles Uptrend context Downtrend context
Candles 1–2 Move DOWN from the high (initial leg) Move UP from the low (initial leg)
Candles 3–4 Move back UP toward the high Move back DOWN toward the low
Candles 5–6 Move DOWN and touch or approach the 0.5 Fib (EQ) Move UP and touch or approach the 0.5 Fib (EQ)
Do at least 2 candles go in the initial direction, 2 return, and 2 touch the equilibrium?
✓ Yes — six candle rule passes
Range is valid on this timeframe. Note this as your confirmed timeframe.
✗ No
Range is not valid on this timeframe. Drop one timeframe and re-check. If no timeframe passes, do not treat this as a range.
Has price returned to the 0.5 Fib (Equilibrium) after the initial leg?
✓ Yes
Range is confirmed. This confirmation comes before a Market Structure Low/High is established. That is expected — range confirmation is earlier.
✗ No — EQ not yet touched
Range is tentative only. Wait for the equilibrium touch. Do not draw the range until confirmed.
What is the highest timeframe on which the six candle rule is satisfied?
→ Record this
This highest valid timeframe is critical. A move that looks like a break on a lower timeframe may appear as just a wick on this higher timeframe — that changes how you classify deviations later.
3
Validate Range Quality (Rationality Check)
›
Does the range look genuinely horizontal / sideways, or does it look V-shaped and sharp?
✓ Wide and extended — clearly sideways
Good range. Continue to Phase 4. The Fibonacci zones will be reliable.
✗ Narrow and V-shaped — looks more like a trend leg
Irrational range. Technically valid by the rules, but not rational to trade on this timeframe.
Action: Drop to a lower timeframe and find a cleaner, more horizontal sub-range. Trade that instead.
V-Shape Test: If the structure forms a tight, narrow V, treat it as market structure (a pool), not a range. If the V is wide with nearly flat tops/bottoms, it qualifies as a range.
4
Map the Range Zones (EQ, Premium, Discount, DL)
›
Fib Level Label Meaning Bias
0.0 Range High Upper boundary of the range SELL zone
0.5 Equilibrium (EQ) Midpoint. Range confirmation trigger. Fair price. Neutral
1.0 Range Low Lower boundary of the range BUY zone
−0.3 / 1.3 Deviation Limit (DL) 30% extension beyond each extreme. The threshold between a deviation and a range break. BREAK threshold
How to draw the DL: On your Fibonacci tool, add custom levels at 1.3 (30% below Range Low) and −0.3 (30% above Range High). These lines define the hard outer limits of deviation territory.
Premium vs. Discount Framework
Zone Location Implication
Premium EQ (0.5) up to Range High (0.0) Price is expensive. Look for distribution / short setups.
Discount EQ (0.5) down to Range Low (1.0) Price is cheap. Look for accumulation / long setups.
5
Wait for a Range Extreme to Be Approached / Breached
›
Where is price relative to the range boundaries right now?
→ Price is approaching or at the Range High
Bearish Setup  Watch for a deviation above the Range High → enter short targeting the Range Low (or at minimum the lower premium zone).
→ Price is approaching or at the Range Low
Bullish Setup  Watch for a deviation below the Range Low → enter long targeting the Range High (or at minimum the upper discount zone).
→ Price is near the Equilibrium (0.5)
No deviation setup yet. Price is at fair value. Wait for it to reach a range extreme before looking for entries.
→ Price is deep inside the Premium zone, trending away from Range High
Track the internal rotation. Identify the most recent local expansion direction and draw a sub-range from that. Do not trade counter to the local expansion until price reaches the opposite extreme.
6
Classify the Breach: Deviation or Range Break?
›

Price has exceeded the Range High or Range Low. Work through these questions in order.

Did price wick beyond the range boundary without closing outside it?
✓ Yes — pure wick
Type 1 Wick Deviation. Highest-confidence deviation type. There was never a close outside — it is unambiguously a deviation. Proceed to Phase 7.
✗ No — price closed outside the boundary
Continue to next question.
Did the very next candle close back inside the range?
✓ Yes — immediate rejection after the close
Type 2 Candle Close Deviation (also called a "Bad Break of Structure").
The breakout failed on the next candle — this is still a deviation. Proceed to Phase 7.
✗ No — price continued to close outside over multiple candles
Continue to next question.
Has any candle closed beyond the Deviation Limit (the 1.3 / −0.3 level)?
✓ Yes — a close beyond the DL
Potential Range Break. This is no longer classified as a deviation. Treat the move as a structural break and reassess the macro trend direction. Do NOT look for a reversal entry against this move.
✗ No — multiple closes outside the range but all within the DL
Still a deviation (multiple bad closes, but all within the DL). Continue to the timeframe check.
Step up to the highest valid timeframe for this range. Does the move look like a wick or a clean break from this vantage point?
→ Looks like a wick or brief excursion on the higher TF
Confirmed Deviation on the higher timeframe. The lower-timeframe closes are noise. Proceed to Phase 7.
→ Looks like a confirmed structural break on the higher TF
Range Break. Do not trade it as a deviation. Reassess macro structure.
Deviation Type Summary
Type Description Confidence
Type 1 — Wick Price wicks beyond the boundary, candle closes back inside. Highest
Type 2 — Bad BOS Candle closes outside, next candle closes back inside immediately. High
Multi-close within DL Multiple closes outside but none beyond the DL. Check highest TF. Moderate — requires TF confirmation
DL exceeded Close beyond the 30% DL level. Range Break — do not trade as deviation
7
Adjust the Range for the Deviation (Extend + Recalculate DL)
›
Did price deviate the Range High?
✓ Yes
Extend the Range High upward to the deviation high (the wick tip or the high close).
Recalculate the DL: the new DL above is 30% of the new (larger) range size above the new Range High.
Did price deviate the Range Low?
✓ Yes
Extend the Range Low downward to the deviation low.
Recalculate the DL: the new DL below is 30% of the new (larger) range size below the new Range Low.
Why extend the range? The deviation extreme becomes the new structural reference point. The DL must be reset to 30% of the updated range so that future deviation classification remains accurate relative to the actual range size.
8
Check Supply / Demand Zone Confluence with the DL
›
Does a demand zone exist near or overlapping the DL below the Range Low?
✓ Yes — demand zone overlaps the DL
Treat the DL as sitting at the bottom of the demand zone, not the DL line itself.
A tap into the demand zone is still a valid deviation even if price briefly closed below the raw DL line.
✗ No — no demand zone near the DL
Use the raw DL line as the hard threshold.
Does a supply zone exist near or overlapping the DL above the Range High?
✓ Yes — supply zone overlaps the DL
Treat the DL as sitting at the top of the supply zone.
A tap into the supply zone is still a valid deviation even if price briefly closed above the raw DL line.
✗ No
Use the raw DL line as the hard threshold.
Key principle: Supply and demand zones are higher-quality structural references than the raw DL percentage. When they conflict, respect the zone boundary over the mechanical DL line — but only within reasonable proximity (the zone should overlap or nearly touch the DL).
9
Determine Trade Bias and Target
›
Deviation Direction Trade Bias Primary Target Minimum Target
Above Range High Short / Sell (Distribution) Range Low Lower portion of the range (below EQ)
Below Range Low Long / Buy (Accumulation) Range High Upper portion of the range (above EQ)
Timeframe Strictness Rules for Entry
Timeframe Deviation Speed Required Tolerance
5m / 10m (Low TF) Quick and sharp. Wick or one bad close, then immediate reversal. Low — be strict
1h / 4h (Mid TF) Moderate. A few candles outside is acceptable. Moderate
Daily / 3D (High TF) Can be extended — multiple candles, deeper excursion. More people get manipulated. High — be more lenient
10
Enter via TCT Schematic (Model 1 or Model 2)
›
The range provides context and direction. The actual entry trigger is a TCT schematic (accumulation or distribution model) that forms after the deviation is confirmed. This is detailed in TCT Lectures 5A, 5B, and 6.
Range role: The deviation at the range extreme tells you where and which direction to look. The TCT schematic (1-tap, 2-tap, 3-tap pattern) tells you when to enter.
Checklist Before Entry
  • Range is confirmed (six candle rule + EQ touch)
  • Range is rational (not V-shaped)
  • Deviation is classified (Type 1 or Type 2, DL not exceeded)
  • Range extended to deviation extreme; DL recalculated
  • Supply/demand zone confluence checked
  • Trade bias determined (long or short)
  • TCT schematic forming or confirmed at the extreme
  • Target identified (opposite range extreme or inner zone)
+
Bonus: Ranges Within Ranges (Internal Structure)
›
Is price currently inside a confirmed macro range but not near either extreme?
✓ Yes
Do not draw market structure trend lines across the entire range.
Instead: identify the most recent expansion / rotation direction inside the range and draw a local sub-range from that move.
What direction was the most recent internal expansion?
→ Most recent expansion was upward (local uptrend)
Draw the local sub-range Fib Top → Bottom. Look for deviation/accumulation at the local range low before targeting the local range high.
→ Most recent expansion was downward (local downtrend)
Draw the local sub-range Fib Bottom → Top. Look for deviation/distribution at the local range high before targeting the local range low.
Hierarchy rule: Higher-timeframe macro range always takes priority. Internal sub-ranges are subordinate. When the macro range extreme is reached, the macro setup overrides any internal sub-range setup.
