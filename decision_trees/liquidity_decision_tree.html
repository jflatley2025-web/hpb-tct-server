TCT Lecture 4 — Liquidity: Decision Tree

Full decision framework extracted from TCT 2024 Mentorship Lecture 4 | Liquidity.
Core mental model: market makers engineer price to stop-loss clusters, fill their position, then reverse to the opposite liquidity pool.

"When I am talking about liquidity I am mostly referring to where the most stop losses are located." — TCT Lecture 4
1
Understand the Core Mechanic (Market Maker Framework)
›
Why Stop Losses = Liquidity
Position TypeStop Loss LocationWhen Triggered CreatesMarket Maker Uses It To
Long position Below a low Sell order (exchange sells their position back) Fill buy/long positions (he needs sellers)
Short position Above a high Buy order (exchange buys back their short) Fill short/sell positions (he needs buyers)
Buy-Side vs Sell-Side Liquidity
BUY-SIDE LIQ
Sits ABOVE highs (Range High, Market Structure Highs, trend line highs). Stop losses of shorts. When swept → buy orders spike price.
DL2 ABOVE ↑
Deviation Limit 2 — 30% extension above Range High. Close above = true break. Wick only = valid grab.
RANGE HIGH
Upper boundary. Buy-side liquidity clusters here.
RANGE BODY
Internal liquidity: higher lows (sell-side) and lower highs (buy-side) within the range.
RANGE LOW
Lower boundary. Sell-side liquidity clusters here.
DL2 BELOW ↓
Deviation Limit 2 — 30% extension below Range Low. Close below = true break. Wick only = valid grab.
SELL-SIDE LIQ
Sits BELOW lows (Range Low, Market Structure Lows, trend line lows). Stop losses of longs. When swept → sell orders dump price.
Core rule: "Markets love to move from liquidity to liquidity." Buy-side swept → expect move to sell-side. Sell-side swept → expect move to buy-side. The target is always the next obvious liquidity pool.
2
Identify All Active Liquidity Pools on Your Chart
›
Pool TypeLocationSideIdentification Rule
Range High Above the Range High Buy-side Confirmed range (EQ touched). Shorts inside range have stops above the high. "The most liquidity will always be above your Range High."
Range Low Below the Range Low Sell-side Confirmed range. Longs inside range have stops below the low. "The most liquidity will always be below your Range Low."
Market Structure High (downtrend) Above each prior swing high in a downtrend Buy-side Shorts entering on each break of structure place stops above the prior high. Trailing stops compound this. "In a downtrend your market structure high will always have the most liquidity."
Market Structure Low (uptrend) Below each prior swing low in an uptrend Sell-side Longs entering on each break of structure place stops below the prior low. "The market structure low in the uptrend will always have the most liquidity."
Trend Line Liquidity Above a descending series of lower highs (or below ascending lows) Buy-side / Sell-side Draw a line connecting highs (downtrend) or lows (uptrend). Each touch = stop cluster. Valid only if no supply backs the highs (or no demand backs the lows).
Liquidity Curve Above a curving series of lower highs (price accelerating too fast for a straight line) Buy-side / Sell-side Curved version of trend line liquidity. Valid only if no demand at each higher low (or no supply at each lower high). Draw a curve connecting points.
Internal Range Liquidity Higher lows inside a range (sell-side) and lower highs inside a range (buy-side) Both sides Longs who bought inside the range put stops below the most recent higher low. Shorts inside put stops above the most recent lower high.
Is there a trend line or liquidity curve forming?
→ Trend line liquidity (straight line connects highs/lows)
Check: Is there no supply backing the highs (for a descending trend line)?
If supply exists at the highs → not a clean liquidity sweep setup.
If no supply → valid trend line liquidity. Do NOT short the lower high. Wait for all highs to be swept.
→ Liquidity curve (cannot connect with straight line — too steep)
Check: Is there no demand at any of the higher lows?
If demand present at any low → not a valid curve (something holds price up).
If no demand → valid curve. Expect a cascading sweep of all highs.
Stacked liquidity rule: Market makers calculate the total stop-loss order value they need. They may need to sweep multiple pools to fill their full position. Example: "Above this high = $10M, that high = $20M, that high = $15M. He needs $45M — he'll push price to trigger all three." Cascades are self-amplifying.
3
Determine Which Side Has the Most Untouched Liquidity
›
Has the Range High been swept one or more times without a DL2 close?
✓ Yes — Range High repeatedly swept (multiple grabs)
Buy-side being exhausted. Market maker is repeatedly filling short positions. Sell-side (Range Low) is the next major target. Anticipate a move downward.
Has the Range Low been swept one or more times without a DL2 close?
✓ Yes — Range Low repeatedly swept
Sell-side being exhausted. Market maker is repeatedly filling long positions. Buy-side (Range High) is the next major target. Anticipate a move upward.
Is one side untouched while the other is being repeatedly swept?
✓ Yes — asymmetric sweep pattern
Highest conviction directional read. The untouched side is the market maker's true target. Each repeated sweep of the other side fills more of his position. When he stops sweeping and accepts back inside → the move to the untouched side begins.
"We deviated the low over and over and over again... liquidity grab one, liquidity grab two, liquidity grab three, liquidity grab four. Each time he was filling his buy positions."
4
Classify the Move: Deviation (Grab) or True Range Break?
›
DL2 Rule: The Deviation Limit 2 (30% extension beyond the Range High or Low) is the hard threshold. A wick through it is acceptable. Only a close beyond it confirms a true structural break.
Did price exceed the Range High or Range Low?
→ Price moved above the Range High
Proceed to DL2 check below. Determine if it's a grab or a break.
→ Price moved below the Range Low
Proceed to DL2 check below. Determine if it's a grab or a break.
Did any candle close beyond the DL2 (30% extension level)?
✗ No — only a wick beyond DL2, or price stayed within DL2
Valid deviation / liquidity grab.
"A wick is completely fine. You need a close above it for it to be invalidated."
The market maker swept the stops. The range is still intact.
✓ Yes — a candle closed beyond the DL2
True range break.
"Did we close above our DL limit? Yes we did, very convincingly. So yes, that is a market structure break."
Do NOT apply liquidity reversal logic. Reassess trend direction.

5
Wait for Acceptance Back Inside the Range
›
After the deviation, has price returned and closed back inside the range?
✓ Yes — acceptance back inside
Liquidity grab confirmed. The deviation was a sweep, not a breakout. Proceed to Phase 6 (path evaluation).
✗ No — price still holding outside the range
Wait. Acceptance back inside is required before applying reversal logic. Without it, the grab is not confirmed.
What Acceptance Confirms
Range High deviated + accepted back inside:
→ Market maker filled short positions using buy-side liquidity. He is now short.
→ The cascade down to Range Low begins. Target = Range Low (sell-side liquidity).
Range Low deviated + accepted back inside:
→ Market maker filled long positions using sell-side liquidity. He is now long.
→ The cascade up to Range High begins. Target = Range High (buy-side liquidity).
SFP (Swing Failure Pattern) — Wick of a Market Structure Low/High:
→ Wick of MS Low (sell-side grabbed) → market maker filled buys → target: opposite MS High.
→ Wick of MS High (buy-side grabbed) → market maker filled shorts → target: opposite MS Low.
"If we get acceptance back inside the range, that could mean a reversal back down to the next liquidity point."
6
Evaluate the Return-to-Zone Path (Clean or Obstructed?)
›
Between the current price (post-grab acceptance) and the target (opposite liquidity pool), is the path clear of active supply/demand zones?
✓ Yes — path contains only liquidity (no active S/D zones)
Clean return to zone. Highest conviction. Expect a fast, cascading move (snowball effect): each stop cluster triggered cascades into the next, amplifying the move.

Explicit rule: extra confirmation when path is clean → "high extra confirmation when we have a return to zone."
✗ No — active demand (for a short) or supply (for a long) sits in the path
Obstructed path. The move may stall or reverse at the intervening zone. Lower conviction. The supply/demand zone may absorb momentum before the target is reached.
Consider: is the S/D zone already mitigated? If yes, path may still be clean.
The Cascade / Snowball Effect
When path is clean (only liquidity between entry and target):

Stop₁ triggered → sell order → price spikes into Stop₂ → Stop₂ triggered → sell order → price spikes into Stop₃ → ... → target reached in a fast, accelerating move.

"That first order getting hit will spike price down to that order and that order and then you have a cascade down of sell pressure basically just by stop losses getting hit."
Key check: For a bearish move (post Range High grab) → is there any unmitigated demand between current price and Range Low? For a bullish move (post Range Low grab) → is there any unmitigated supply between current price and Range High? If yes, downgrade conviction.
7
Assess the Compounding Effect (Retail Trap = Extra Fuel)
›
After the Range High deviation, are retail traders likely trying to long the perceived "break of structure"?
✓ Yes — retail longs are entering above the Range High
Extra fuel for the short. These longs place their stops below the new perceived higher low. When the market reverses back inside the range, their stops trigger → more sell orders → accelerates the cascade down.

"People trying to long the higher low... their stop loss will get hit — which will just add extra fuel to the fire."
→ After Range Low deviation: retail shorts entering below the Range Low
Extra fuel for the long. These shorts have stops above the new lower high. When market reverses inside, their stops trigger → more buy orders → accelerates the cascade up.
Trailing stops compound the effect: "Sometimes people even trail stop losses meaning it's double the amount on one low." Each market structure point may contain more liquidity than it appears.
8
Wait for TCT Schematic Entry Confirmation
›
Critical rule: Liquidity analysis tells you the direction and target. It does NOT give you the entry trigger. Entry is via a TCT schematic (Model 1 or Model 2) confirmed after acceptance back inside the range. Covered in Lectures 5A, 5B, and 6.
Has a TCT schematic confirmation formed after acceptance back inside the range?
✓ Yes — TCT schematic confirmed
Enter the trade. All conditions met: liquidity grab → DL2 check passed → acceptance back inside → path evaluated → TCT confirmation received.
✗ No — no TCT confirmation yet
Wait. Do not enter on liquidity analysis alone. The schematic is the trigger.

9
Pre-Trade Checklist — All Must Pass
›
  • Range confirmed (from Lecture 2: six candle rule + EQ touch)
  • All liquidity pools identified and mapped (Range High/Low, MS highs/lows, trend line / curve)
  • Determined which side has the most untouched / repeatedly-swept liquidity
  • Price has moved beyond a range extreme (Range High or Low)
  • DL2 check: no candle has closed beyond the 30% extension — classified as a deviation, not a break
  • Acceptance back inside the range confirmed
  • Path from entry to target evaluated — is it clean (only liquidity) or obstructed (active S/D)?
  • Retail trap assessed — are trapped longs/shorts adding fuel to the move?
  • TCT schematic entry confirmation received (Model 1 or Model 2)
  • Target identified: opposite liquidity pool (Range Low or Range High)
+
Bonus: Trend Line & Liquidity Curve Trading Workflow
›
Trend Line Liquidity — Do's and Don'ts
Retail Approach (Wrong)TCT Approach (Correct)
Short the lower high as it touches the descending trend line Wait — the market maker needs to sweep those highs to fill short positions. Do not trade against him.
Long the higher low on the ascending trend line Wait — the market maker needs to sweep those lows to fill long positions. Do not trade against him.
Set stop loss just above/below the trend line Recognize that YOUR stop is the liquidity being targeted. Adjust accordingly.
Trend Line Liquidity — Correct Sequence
  1. Draw the descending trend line connecting lower highs (or ascending line of higher lows)
  2. Verify: no supply backs the descending highs / no demand backs the ascending lows
  3. Wait for price to sweep all trend line highs (buy-side) or lows (sell-side)
  4. Confirm acceptance back inside the range (DL2 not closed beyond)
  5. Evaluate path to target — is it clear?
  6. Wait for TCT schematic confirmation
  7. Target: next major sell-side pool (after highs swept) or buy-side pool (after lows swept)
Liquidity Curve Validity Check
For a downward curve (series of lower highs): is there no supply backing each high?
✓ No supply at the highs — curve is valid
Valid liquidity curve. Each high will be swept. Expect a sharp cascade once the sweep completes.
✗ Supply exists at one or more highs
Not a clean liquidity curve. Something is holding price down. Do not expect a clean multi-sweep.
For an upward curve (series of higher lows): is there no demand at each low?
✓ No demand at the lows — curve is valid
Valid liquidity curve. Each low will be swept. Expect a sharp cascade down once the sweep completes.
✗ Demand exists at one or more lows
Not a clean curve. Demand zones can hold price up at each low, stalling or absorbing the sweep.
