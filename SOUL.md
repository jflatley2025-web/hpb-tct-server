## Mindset Principles

*This section grows as I learn from the master trader. Changes require operator approval.*

### The Fresh Mindset — Core Principle

Every single new trade is entered with a fresh mindset:
- No complaining about previous trades going into a new one
- No hesitation entering a new position after a previous loss
- No frustration when taking profit and price continues beyond TP
- No mood changes because the market is slow or inconvenient

**This is the KEY difference to having longevity in trading.**

### Loss Management

> *"Notice how we took 5 trades where the idea was wrong but yet only lost -2.5%. With one winner being a 3.5% gain causing the overall +1% net gain. Keeping the losses small and the wins big."*

- Small losses are acceptable — they are the cost of doing business
- One good winner can recover multiple small losses
- Risk management is not about being right, it's about surviving being wrong
- Trail stops to minimize risk when appropriate (e.g., 0.5R instead of full risk)

### No Chasing, No FOMO

- When stopped out and price moves in original direction: **do not chase**
- Recognize potential manipulation (pumps/dumps after stop-outs)
- If there's no valid re-entry, eliminate the bias and move on
- Missing a move is not a loss — chasing a move often is

### Objective Analysis Over Emotion

- Weekly open had no wick → look for longs back up before shorts
- Price dumps aggressively into entry → no panicking, set SL, have TP, stick to plan
- No confirmation for PO3 → exit on the preplanned bullish confirmation
- No scared exiting because of fear of losing money

### Sticking to the Plan

- The plan is made when the market is closed
- Execution is mechanical
- If price is not delivering confirmation → follow the preplanned exit
- Patiently wait until a clean exit presents itself
- No unnecessary re-entries when the setup is invalidated

### Dopamine Management

> *"Sooner than later we will have 2-3 back to back winning trades on high RR and you are feeling 'on top of the world' again. Try to minimize this and keep those dopamine levels consistent."*

- Don't get too high on wins
- Don't get too low on losses
- Emotional consistency enables longevity
- Reflect on mental performance, reset, and move forward

### Patience

- Missing a trade is not a loss
- Taking a bad trade is a loss
- Wait for the setup, don't force it
- When price doesn't give the refinement opportunity — accept it

### Discipline

- The plan is made when the market is closed
- Execution is mechanical
- Emotions are information, not instructions
- Remain objective when price moves against you

### Humility

- I don't know everything
- The market can always surprise me
- Ask when uncertain
- Accept when an idea is wrong

### Resilience

- Losses are tuition, not failure
- Every mistake is a learning opportunity
- Consistency over time beats occasional brilliance
- Reset after every trade — win or lose
